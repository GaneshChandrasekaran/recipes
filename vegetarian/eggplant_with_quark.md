**Ingredients**
1. 3 eggplants
2. Salt
3. 2 small onions
4. 2 tomatoes
5. 120 ml oil
6. 2 tbsp oil
7. 2-3 tbsp tomato paste
8. Cayenne pepper
9. Sugar
10. 1 tsp ground coriander (or cumin)
11. 2 garlic cloves
12. 250 g low-fat quark
13. 2-3 stems mint


**Steps**
1. Wash and halve the eggplants. Prick the flesh several times with a fork, sprinkle generously with salt, and let them sit for about 30 minutes.
2. Peel and finely dice the onions. Wash and trim the tomatoes, then slice them thinly.
3. Trim the ends off the eggplants, pat the cut sides dry with paper towels, and slice into about 0.5 cm thick pieces.
4. Heat 120 ml oil in a large covered frying pan. Fry the eggplant slices in batches, turning occasionally, until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
5. Heat 2 tbsp oil in the same pan. Sauté onions for about 2 minutes until translucent.
6. Stir tomato paste with 125 ml water and deglaze the pan. Bring to a boil. Season with salt, cayenne pepper, a pinch of sugar, and ground coriander.
7. Skim off half the sauce and reserve it. Layer eggplant slices in the pan, spread tomatoes on top, pour over the reserved sauce, cover, and simmer on low heat for 5-10 minutes.
8. Peel and finely chop garlic. Mix quark, garlic, and 2-3 tbsp water in a bowl. Season with salt and a pinch of sugar.
9. Wash mint, shake dry, pluck leaves, and roughly chop.
10. Arrange the cooked eggplant in a casserole dish or shallow bowl. Spoon some quark mixture on top, sprinkle with mint, and serve remaining quark on the side. Serve with flatbread or pita if desired.
